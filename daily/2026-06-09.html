In 2 Days World Cup 2026 09 Jun 2026

48 Nations. Three Continents. The Biggest World Cup in History Starts Thursday.

The 2026 FIFA World Cup opens on June 11th with Mexico hosting South Africa at the Estadio Azteca — the first of 104 matches across 16 cities in the United States, Canada, and Mexico. It is the first edition to feature 48 teams, expanding the group stage to 12 groups of four. The USA begin their campaign on June 12th at SoFi Stadium against Paraguay. Belgium enter Group F alongside Egypt, Iran, and New Zealand, opening on June 15th. The final is scheduled for July 19th at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey.

Title Race Odds 09 Jun 2026

Spain and France Co-Favourites at 16% Each. England, Argentina, Brazil Close Behind.

Prediction markets have Spain and France essentially tied as tournament favourites at roughly 16% implied probability each. Spain leads most bookmakers at +475, with France having slipped slightly after a 2–1 defeat to Ivory Coast in a friendly on June 4th. England sit third at +650, Brazil fourth at +800, and defending champions Argentina — led by Lionel Messi — at +950. Germany (+1300) are considered a tier below. The bracket, if it goes chalk, sets up a potential Spain vs England final — a rematch of Euro 2024, which nobody wants to simulate twice.

The Last Dance 09 Jun 2026

Messi. Ronaldo. Six World Cups Each. One Last Shot at History.

Lionel Messi, 38, and Cristiano Ronaldo, 41, will become the first players in history to appear at six different World Cups — two decades on from their 2006 debuts. Messi arrives as defending champion and sits three goals behind Klose's all-time record of 16. Ronaldo confirmed this will be his last: "Definitely, yes." He has never played in a World Cup final. Portugal face DR Congo on June 17th. A quarter-final path could yet set up the showdown the sport has waited 20 years to see.

Squads 02 Jun 2026

1,248 Players. 48 Squads. Craig Gordon (43) to Gilberto Mora (17).

All 48 final squads were officially confirmed by FIFA on June 2nd. A record 1,248 players are eligible for 104 matches, of which 891 are competing at a World Cup for the first time. Scotland's Craig Gordon, at 43, is the oldest player in the tournament; Mexico's Gilberto Mora, at 17, the youngest. Lamine Yamal, Endrick, and Jude Bellingham represent the next generation stepping into the spotlight. Robert Lewandowski and Rodrygo both missed out. Guillermo Ochoa, 40, returns for a sixth tournament after first-choice Angel Malagon suffered an Achilles rupture in March — a career arc that would be implausible in fiction.

Race ResultMonaco GP · Round 608 Jun 2026

Antonelli Wins Monaco in the Chaos. Five Wins from Six Races. The Gap Is 66 Points.

Kimi Antonelli delivered a dominant lights-to-flag victory in a chaotic Monaco Grand Prix to claim his fifth win from six races. Verstappen stalled on the front row and retired on the opening lap. Leclerc crashed out when the circuit surface broke up with ten laps remaining. George Russell collected a drive-through for pit lane speeding, failed to serve it, received a second penalty, and finished outside the points. Hamilton finished second. A post-race penalty for Perez handed Fernando Alonso Aston Martin's first point of the season. Monaco methodically selects its chaos. This year it simply committed.

Player AnalysisGame 3 · 08 Jun 2026

Wembanyama: 32 Pts, 8 Reb, 6 Ast, 3 Blk, 2 Stl. The Second-Youngest 30–5–5 in Finals History.

Victor Wembanyama delivered the best individual performance of this Finals: 32 points on 11-of-18 shooting, eight rebounds, six assists, three blocks and two steals. Twenty-two of those points came at the rim or from the free-throw line. He became the second-youngest player to post a 30-5-5 in the Finals, behind Magic Johnson. Stephon Castle added 23 points including a clutch three-pointer with under two minutes left. De'Aaron Fox's late runner extended the lead to five. The Spurs scored 123.7 points per 100 possessions in the second half.
