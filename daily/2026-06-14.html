53 years, one floater_

The New York Knicks are champions again, dragged across the line by a 45-point masterpiece from Jalen Brunson. And the day doesn't stop: the World Cup's favourites are wobbling, and this afternoon Antonelli's streak meets Russell's pole in Barcelona.

Day 008 Sunday · 14 June 2026 sports.nodeshield.net
NBA Finals · Champions

The Comeback Knicks become the Champion Knicks

For the first time since 1973, New York rules the NBA. The Knicks beat the San Antonio Spurs 94-90 in Game 5 to take the series 4-1, and they did it the only way they knew how all post-season: from behind. Down by 16 on the night, New York leaned on Jalen Brunson, who poured in 45 points — a Knicks Finals record — including 13 straight in the fourth quarter. His go-ahead floater with 65 seconds left settled it.

Brunson was the unanimous Finals MVP, and the manner of it puts him in rare company: only Michael Jordan, Giannis Antetokounmpo and Bob Pettit had previously scored 45+ in a title-clinching game. The defining stat of the whole series, though, is collective madness — the Knicks erased double-digit deficits in all four of their wins, capped by the largest comeback in Finals history (a 29-point hole) in Game 4.

"After 53 years, your Knicks are finally NBA champions once again." — and Madison Square Garden exhaled half a century at once.

Spare a thought for Victor Wembanyama (19 pts, 14 reb, 5 blocks), who called the defeat the biggest learning moment of his life. The Spurs led the first quarter of every single game and still lost the war. They'll be back. New York, finally, didn't have to wait for "next year."

FIFA World Cup 2026

Favourites stutter, minnows bite

Day 3 had a clear theme: the big names didn't have it their own way. Brazil were held 1-1 by Morocco in New Jersey — Ismael Saibari struck first, Vinícius Júnior rescued the point, and the Seleção looked, in one writer's phrase, a work in progress. Switzerland dominated Qatar with 23 shots and still drew 1-1 to a last-gasp equaliser. Then the underdogs pounced.

Scotland beat Haiti 1-0 on a John McGinn finish — their first World Cup win since 1990, and enough to top Group C above Brazil. Australia stunned Türkiye 2-0, Nestory Irankunda and Connor Metcalfe doing the damage to leave the Socceroos level with the USA in Group D. Two of the day's three "small" sides walked away happier than the giants.

Watch Netherlands v Japan: a tactically fascinating clash of a heavyweight and one of the tournament's most improved sides, and the kind of fixture an expanded field throws up on a Sunday afternoon in June.

Formula 1 · Barcelona GP Today

Russell's pole, Antonelli's streak — something gives today

Round 7 runs this afternoon at the Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya, and for once there's a crack in the Mercedes monolith. George Russell took pole — his third of the season — edging Ferrari's Lewis Hamilton by 0.064s, with championship leader Kimi Antonelli only third. It's the first time all year Antonelli has started off the front row.

The stakes are simple. Antonelli arrives on five straight wins and a 66-point lead; another victory and the title race is effectively a formality by midsummer. But starting third, with his own resurgent team-mate on pole and a hungry Hamilton alongside, this is the first real invitation for someone to interrupt the procession. Charles Leclerc, meanwhile, starts tenth after crashing out of Q3 — Ferrari's running theme of one car flying, one in the wall.

Around the Grounds

Elsewhere on a stacked Sunday

It's one of those rare days when three sports peak at once: an NBA champion crowned overnight, four World Cup fixtures stretching into the small hours, and an F1 race in the afternoon. If you're building a watch-list, the order is simple — relive the Knicks, settle in for Netherlands v Japan, then Barcelona lights-out.

Quietly continuing in the background, Taiwan's TPBL finals roll on between the Formosa Dreamers and New Taipei Kings — proof that the basketball calendar never really sleeps, even on the morning after a coronation in San Antonio.
